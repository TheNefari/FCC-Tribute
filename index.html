Stephen William Hawking

Discoverer of Hawking radiation

Stephen Hawking smiling, taken at NASA's StarChild Learning Center

Summary of Stephen Hawking's Life:

  • 1942 - born in Oxford, England
  • 1950 - attended St Albans High School for Girls
  • 1958 - built a computer from block parts with the help of Dikran Tahta
  • 1959 - began university education at University College in Oxford
  • 1962 - began graduate work at Trinity Hall in Cambridge followed by a diagnosis of motor neural disease
  • 1965 - wrote thesis about Properties of Expanding Universes
  • 1966 - obtained PhD degree in applied mathematics and theoretical physics
  • 1970 - published a proof of the beginning of the universe as a singularity with Roger Penrose
  • 1973 - showed that according to the uncertainty principle, rotating black holes emit particles, known today as Hawking radiation
  • 1975 - academically senior post as reader in gravitational physics in Cambridge
  • 1981 - presented work suggesting no boundary or beginning or ending to the universe at a Vatican conference
  • 1988 - published "A Brief History of Time", a book about the universe, accessible to the general public
  • 1997 - starred in Stephen Hawking's Universe, a television series with a companion book
  • 2007 - published George's Secret Key to the Universe, a children's book explaining theoretical physics in a simple manner, together with his daughter Lucy
  • 2009 - had a party for time-travellers, but only told people after it was over
  • 2015 - helped to launch a search for extraterrestrial life called "Breakthrough Initiatives"
  • 2018 - published his last pater "A smooth exit from eternal inflation?"
  • 2018 - died in Cambridge, England, at the age of 76

“We are just an advanced breed of monkeys on a minor planet of a very average star. But we can understand the Universe. That makes us something very special.”
― Stephen Hawking
